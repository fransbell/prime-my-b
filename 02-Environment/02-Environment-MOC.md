**Key Insights**:
- Northern Thailand's cool season (Nov–Feb) provides the temperature differential Arabica needs for quality cherry development, while the distinct dry period triggers synchronized flowering
- At 18–20°N latitude, Northern Thailand can produce quality Arabica at 800–1,700m — lower than equatorial regions — due to subtropical cooling
- Chiang Rai is the wettest coffee province (~1,700–2,172mm annual rainfall); Chiang Mai is the driest (~1,100mm)
- Shade trees can reduce canopy temperature by 3–5°C — equivalent to gaining 450–830m of altitude
- Climate change is shifting Arabica suitability upward by ~150–167m per 1°C of warming; adaptation through shade, irrigation, and resistant varieties is essential
