# Seed Sources & Nurseries in Thailand

> **Summary**: Comprehensive directory of government and private coffee seedling sources in Thailand, with varieties, pricing, contact details, certification standards, and planting guidelines for Northern Thailand.

---

## Overview

Sourcing quality coffee seedlings is one of the most critical decisions a farmer makes, as the variety and genetic quality of planting material determines productivity for 15-30+ years. In Northern Thailand, the government (DOA, DOAE, Royal Project) provides free or subsidized seedlings of registered varieties, while private nurseries offer convenience and specialty varieties. This guide covers all major sources, from the DOA's Chiang Mai 80 distribution program to specialty nurseries selling Geisha and Pacamara. The key rule: always verify the variety authenticity, as mislabeled seedlings can result in years of lost income.

## Government Seed Sources

### Department of Agriculture (DOA)

The DOA is the primary agency responsible for coffee variety development, registration, and seedling distribution in Thailand. It operates research centers and highland agricultural stations that breed, test, and distribute certified coffee seedlings.

| Facility | Location | Role | Contact |
|----------|----------|------|---------|
| **Horticulture Research Institute** | Bangkok (HQ) | National coffee variety R&D, variety registration | www.doa.go.th/hort |
| **Chiang Mai Agricultural Research Center** | Chiang Mai | Arabica coffee breeding, Chiang Mai 80 & DOA Chiang Mai 1 development | www.doa.go.th/hc/cmrarc |
| **Khao Kho Highland Agricultural Research Center** | Phetchabun | Arabica coffee seedling distribution, macadamia | www.doa.go.th/hc/khaokho |
| **Chumphon Horticultural Research Center** | Chumphon | Robusta coffee breeding — Chumphon 1 & Chumphon 2 | www.doa.go.th/hc/chumphon |
| **Loei Horticultural Research Center** | Loei | Catimor/Chiang Mai 80 variety testing & seedling | www.doa.go.th/hort |

**DOA-Registered Arabica Varieties:**
- **Chiang Mai 80**: Catimor-type hybrid (CIFC 7963-13-28 x SL28 backcross), registered 2007. Rust-resistant, compact bush, high yield, cup score 6.5-7/10. Most widely planted Arabica in Northern Thailand. Recommended for altitudes 700-1,200+ masl.
- **DOA Chiang Mai 1**: Newer variety (registered approximately 2024), cross of Mundo Novo 1535/33 x H.W. 26/14 from Portuguese coffee rust research center. Higher yield than Chiang Mai 80 (approximately 3.8-4.0 kg cherry/tree vs 2 kg for older varieties), rust-resistant, cup score 76.75/100, smooth mouthfeel with long aftertaste. Recommended for 1,200+ masl.

**DOA-Registered Robusta Varieties:**
- **Chumphon 1**: Selected from local germplasm since 1989, yields more than 2 kg seed/tree/year
- **Chumphon 2**: Imported from France/Togo via Nestle (Thailand) in 1999, Congolese group

**Seedling Distribution:** DOA Highland Agricultural Research Centers periodically distribute free seedlings (Chiang Mai 80, DOA Chiang Mai 1) to registered farmers. Example: Khao Kho center distributed 750 macadamia cuttings and Chiang Mai 80 seedlings in April 2026.

**DOA Plant Variety Registration Portal**: https://doaplant.doa.go.th — Searchable database of all registered varieties with PDF fact sheets.

### Department of Agricultural Extension (DOAE)

DOAE manages the Arabica Coffee Seedling Production Project through its Highland Agricultural Extension Centers. Key operations include:

- Produces Chiang Mai 80 seedlings in 2 rounds annually (October sowing and February sowing)
- Seeds germinate in approximately 45 days
- Seedlings grown to 4-5 pairs of true leaves before distribution
- Distributed through provincial agricultural extension offices

**Contact:** Provincial Agricultural Extension Offices in Chiang Mai, Chiang Rai, Mae Hong Son, Nan, Lampang, Tak. Website: www.doae.go.th

### Royal Project Foundation

Founded in 1969 under H.M. King Bhumibol's initiative to replace opium cultivation with sustainable cash crops. The Royal Project has been the single most important force in developing Arabica coffee as a highland crop in Thailand.

- From 1971, produced over 36 coffee varieties, selecting the best 14
- In 2021, 6 varieties were officially registered as Royal Project Foundation varieties (RPF-C3, RPF-C4, and others)
- Annual coffee production approximately 500 tons across 22 Royal Project areas
- Registered varieties feature compact bush (approximately 1.5m), short internodes, recommended spacing 1.5x2m
- Average cherry yield: 3.815-4.030 kg/tree (significantly higher than traditional varieties at approximately 2 kg/tree)
- Distribution primarily to farmers within Royal Project network; not typically sold commercially

**Key Development Centers:** Mae La Noi (Mae Hong Son), Mae Poon Luang, Inthanin (Doi Inthanon area), Khun Wang (Chiang Mai), Pang Da (Chiang Mai)

**Contact:** Website: www.royalproject.org | Facebook: Royal Project Foundation

### Highland Research and Development Institute (HRDI)

Established as a public organization in 2005. HRDI focuses on sustainable highland development and Arabica coffee R&D.

- Training Courses: "Arabica Coffee in Highland" workshop series
- Knowledge Management: Comprehensive coffee knowledge base at hkm.hrdi.or.th
- Joint Variety Development: Works with Royal Project on RPF-C3, RPF-C4, and new variety selection
- Published guide: "General Information about Coffee" covering seed selection, nursery management, planting

**Contact:** Website: www.hrdi.or.th | Knowledge base: hkm.hrdi.or.th | Location: Chiang Mai

### NECTEC / NSTDA Agricultural Programs

The National Science and Technology Development Agency (NSTDA) and NECTEC provide technology and innovation support for Thai coffee.

- **AGRITEC Institute**: Manages agricultural technology transfer, including coffee. Website: www.nstda.or.th/agritec
- **BIOTEC x DOAE MOU (2024)**: Cooperation agreement to promote quality coffee production; BIOTEC provides biotechnology tools for variety authentication and quality testing
- **What2Grow NECTEC**: YouTube channel with coffee farming educational content
- **Smart Farm Technology**: Digital agriculture tools including coffee zoning, precision farming sensors

**Contact:** NSTDA: www.nstda.or.th | NECTEC: www.nectec.or.th | BIOTEC: www.biotec.or.th

### Doi Tung Development Project

Founded in 1988 by HRH Princess Srinagarindara (the Princess Mother) under the Mae Fah Luang Foundation. Transformed opium-producing areas in Chiang Rai's Golden Triangle into sustainable coffee farming communities.

- Maintains mother plant orchards for seed production
- Produces seedlings for the Doi Tung farming community (approximately 30 villages, 10,000+ people)
- Focuses on integrated shade-grown coffee with macadamia
- Seedlings primarily for internal community use, not typically sold externally
- Altitude: 800-1,400 masl | Coffee Type: 100% Arabica

**Contact:** Website: www.doitung.com | Facebook: DoiTung Coffee

## Certification & Seed Quality Standards

### DOA Variety Registration System

The DOA registration process ensures variety authenticity through: (1) variety development through breeding or selection, (2) multi-location field trials by DOA research centers, (3) data collection on yield, disease resistance, cup quality, (4) submission to DOA Plant Variety Committee, (5) if approved, named per DOA convention (e.g., DOA Chiang Mai 1), and (6) publication on doaplant.doa.go.th.

**Currently Registered Coffee Varieties:**
- Arabica: Chiang Mai 80, DOA Chiang Mai 1, RPF-C3, RPF-C4, plus 2 more Royal Project varieties
- Robusta: Chumphon 1, Chumphon 2

### How to Verify Authentic Variety Seedlings

1. **DOA Registration Check**: Verify variety name at doaplant.doa.go.th
2. **Source Verification**: Buy from DOA research centers, Royal Project centers, or DOAE-registered nurseries
3. **Morphological Identification**: Chiang Mai 80 has compact bush, short internodes; both green-tip and red-tip variants exist
4. **Molecular Verification**: BIOTEC/NSTDA can perform DNA fingerprinting for variety authentication
5. **DOAE Seedling Production Program**: Seedlings from government programs use verified mother trees
6. **Practical Tip**: Reputable nurseries like CoffeeDeep guarantee 100% authentic Catimor/Chiang Mai 80

### Coffee Seed Import Regulations

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Import Permit** | Required before importation — apply via DOA electronic system |
| **Phytosanitary Certificate** | Must accompany shipment, issued by exporting country's government |
| **Prohibited Items** | Coffee plants/seeds from certain countries may be prohibited |
| **Quarantine Station** | Must enter through designated plant quarantine stations |
| **Post-Entry Quarantine** | May be required for live plants |
| **Application Form** | แบบ พ.ก. 2 (Import Permit) or พ.ก. 4 (Transit Permit) |

Coffee seed/plant imports are treated as high-risk (potential pest/disease carriers). Coffee leaf rust (Hemileia vastatrix) and coffee berry borer are major quarantine concerns.

## Planting Best Practices

### Recommended Seedling Age for Transplanting

| Species | Optimal Age | Minimum Height | True Leaf Pairs |
|---------|-------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Arabica** | 8-12 months | 20+ cm | 4-5 pairs |
| **Robusta** | 6-14 months | 20+ cm | 4-5 pairs |

### Best Planting Season in Northern Thailand

| Month | Season | Suitability |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| **May** | Early rainy season | OPTIMAL — Rain begins, soil moist |
| **June** | Rainy season | OPTIMAL — Consistent rainfall |
| **July** | Rainy season | Good — Sufficient rain |
| **August** | Mid rainy season | Acceptable — Seedlings may be smaller |
| **September** | Late rainy season | Risky — Less time to establish before dry season |
| **October-March** | Dry/cool season | NOT recommended — Frost risk at high altitude |

**Key Rule**: Plant at the START of the rainy season (May-June) so seedlings have 4-5 months of rainfall to establish before the dry/cool season. Seedlings sell out quickly when rain starts — order by April.

### Seed Germination Rates

| Variety | Germination Time | Germination Rate | Notes |
|---------|-----------------|------------------|-------|
| **Arabica (general)** | 42-56 days | Approximately 70-80% (fresh seeds) | Seeds lose viability after 2-3 months |
| **Catimor/Chiang Mai 80** | Approximately 45 days | Good with fresh seed | DOAE: approximately 45 days to seedling stage |
| **Old seeds (6+ months)** | Significantly reduced | Less than 50% | Viability drops sharply after 3 months |

### Grafted vs Seed-Propagated Coffee

| Aspect | Seed-Propagated | Grafted |
|--------|----------------|---------|
| **Genetic Consistency** | Variable | Clonal — exact copy of scion parent |
| **Cost** | Lower (5-25 THB/seedling) | Higher (labor-intensive grafting) |
| **Availability in Thailand** | Widely available | Limited — experimental/specialty only |
| **Common Practice in N. Thailand** | Overwhelmingly seed-propagated | Not commonly available |

**Thailand Context:** Grafted coffee plants are NOT commonly available or used in Thailand. The market is dominated by seed-propagated seedlings. Grafting research exists at Maejo University, Chiang Mai, but has not been widely adopted commercially.

## Practical Recommendations

1. **For New Farmers**: Start with Chiang Mai 80 from a reputable nursery (CoffeeDeep or DOA centers). It's the most proven, rust-resistant, and widely supported variety in Northern Thailand.
2. **For Specialty Coffee**: Consider DOA Chiang Mai 1 (higher cup score, better taste profile) or Royal Project RPF-C3/C4 (higher yield). For ultra-premium, source Geisha/Catuai/Pacamara from Sensation Coffee Roasters.
3. **For Robusta**: Source Chumphon 1 or Chumphon 2 from the Chumphon Horticultural Research Center.
4. **Bulk Orders (1,500+ trees)**: Contact CoffeeDeep (090-372-9432) for free shipping, or The Coffee Thai for free delivery in Northern provinces.
5. **Budget Planning**: At 2x3m spacing (267 trees/rai) x 5-10 THB/tree = 1,335-2,670 THB/rai for seedlings alone. Add land preparation, shade trees, fertilizer for first year.
6. **Timing is Critical**: Order seedlings by April for May-June planting. Seedlings sell out quickly when the rainy season starts.
7. **Verify Your Source**: Buy from DOA/DOAE-registered suppliers. For variety authentication, seeds from CMU (Chiang Mai University) or DOA research centers are most reliable.
8. **Community Support**: Join the Facebook group "กลุ่มปลูกกาแฟอาราบิก้า โรบัสต้า โกโก้ ทางภาคเหนือ" for farmer-to-farmer advice, seedling sourcing, and market information.
