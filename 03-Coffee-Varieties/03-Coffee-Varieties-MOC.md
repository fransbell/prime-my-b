**Key Insights**:
- Altitude is the primary determinant — above 800m favors Arabica (Catimor, Typica), below 600m favors Robusta. The 600-800m zone is the decision boundary. Chiang Mai 80 (a Catimor-type selection backcrossed with SL28) accounts for 70-80% of Northern Thai Arabica, while Chumphon clones dominate Thai Robusta. A "Robusta Renaissance" is underway with GI-registered Robusta seeing 7.5x price increases
- Grafting Arabica onto Robusta rootstock (Chumphon clones) can expand Arabica cultivation 200-400m below its natural elevation range, opening the 600-800m transitional zone to Arabica production with 70-90% graft success rates and 18-24 month time-to-first-harvest
- Different varieties require different sensor thresholds — Catimor/Chiang Mai 80 tolerates higher humidity (CLR resistance) but is more heat-sensitive (>28°C stress), while Typica needs tighter humidity monitoring but tolerates a wider temperature range; the Decision Logic Engine must parameterize by variety for accurate alerts
