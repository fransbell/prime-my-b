# Variety Selection Decision Guide

> **Summary**: A data-driven decision framework to help Northern Thailand coffee farmers select the optimal variety based on altitude, climate, budget, and market goals. All prices in THB, yields in kg green bean/rai.

---

## Overview

Altitude is the primary determinant of which coffee variety will thrive in Northern Thailand. Above 800m, Arabica is the natural choice; below 600m, Robusta dominates. The critical decision zone is 600-800m, where site-specific factors like slope aspect, shade availability, and market target determine whether Arabica or Robusta will be more profitable. This guide provides a systematic framework for making the variety decision, including financial projections, climate change considerations, and case studies from successful Northern Thailand coffee communities.

---

## Decision Matrix — Altitude to Variety

### Zone A: Above 1,000m — Premium Arabica Territory

**Northern Thailand examples**: Doi Chang (1,000-1,700m), Doi Tung peak (1,000-1,400m), Doi Wawi (1,200-1,500m), Doi Pang Khon (1,200-1,400m), Doi Inthanon (1,200-2,565m)

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **1st Choice** | **Typica** — heirloom quality, SCA 82-86, best terroir expression at 1,200m+ |
| **2nd Choice** | **Chiang Mai 80** — Catimor x SL28 backcross, leaf rust resistant, SCA 80-84 |
| **3rd Choice** | **Geisha** — highest cup quality SCA 85-92+, but low yield, high cost |
| **4th Choice** | **Catimor H528/21 or H528/46** — rust resistant, reliable yield, SCA 76-80 |
| **Expected Yield** | 80-200 kg green bean/rai (national avg approximately 89 kg/rai; well-managed up to 200) |
| **Market Price** | Commodity: 90-120 THB/kg; Specialty: 280-400 THB/kg; Premium: 500-1,000+ THB/kg |
| **ROI Timeline** | 4-5 years to first profit |

---

### Zone B: 800-1,000m — Arabica Sweet Spot

**Northern Thailand examples**: Doi Tung (800-1,400m range), Mae La Noi (Mae Hong Son, approximately 900m), Chiang Dao (approximately 850m), Fang (approximately 800m)

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **1st Choice** | **Chiang Mai 80** — specifically bred for this altitude range, rust resistant, SCA 80-84 |
| **2nd Choice** | **Catimor (H528 series)** — most widely planted, rust resistant, SCA 76-80 |
| **3rd Choice** | **Caturra** — compact growth, good yield, SCA 79-83, moderate rust resistance |
| **4th Choice** | **Catuai** — high yield, compact, SCA 78-82, needs shade management |
| **Expected Yield** | 100-180 kg green bean/rai |
| **Market Price** | Commodity: 80-110 THB/kg; Specialty: 200-350 THB/kg |
| **ROI Timeline** | 3-4 years to first profit |

---

### Zone C: 600-800m — Decision Boundary (Arabica vs Robusta)

**Northern Thailand examples**: Chiang Mai lowlands (approximately 600-700m), Lampang valleys (approximately 600m), parts of Mae Taeng (approximately 650m)

| Factor | Arabica Option | Robusta Option |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Best Variety** | Catimor (rust resistant only) | Chumphon clone or FRT-series |
| **Expected Yield** | 80-120 kg/rai (lower quality) | 200-350 kg/rai |
| **Cup Quality** | SCA 74-79 (below specialty) | SCA 76-82 (specialty Robusta possible) |
| **Market Price** | 70-100 THB/kg (commodity) | 50-90 THB/kg (commodity); 150-250 THB/kg (specialty) |
| **ROI Timeline** | 4-5 years | 3-4 years |

**Decision Framework at 600-800m:**

| Choose Arabica if... | Choose Robusta if... |
|----------------------|---------------------|
| North-facing slope (cooler) | South-facing slope (warmer) |
| Shade trees available | Open field |
| Target specialty market | Target commodity/high-volume market |
| Higher budget, higher risk tolerance | Lower budget, steady income preferred |
| Experienced with Arabica | New to coffee farming |

**Critical Note:** Climate change is pushing this boundary UPWARD; areas that were 800m suitable are becoming marginal. If choosing Arabica at this altitude, shade is NON-NEGOTIABLE.

---

### Zone D: 400-600m — Robusta Territory

**Northern Thailand examples**: Phayao lowlands (approximately 500m), Nan valley floors (approximately 400-500m), Uttaradit hills (approximately 450m)

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **1st Choice** | **Chumphon clone** — locally adapted, proven in Southern Thailand, SCA 74-80 |
| **2nd Choice** | **FRT-series** — Nestle-developed, proven in Thai conditions |
| **Expected Yield** | 250-400 kg green bean/rai |
| **Market Price** | Commodity: 45-80 THB/kg; Specialty Robusta: 150-280 THB/kg |
| **ROI Timeline** | 3-4 years |

---

### Zone E: 200-400m — Lowland Robusta

**Northern Thailand examples**: Phitsanulok lowlands (approximately 200-300m), Phetchabun foothills (approximately 300m), Tak lowlands (approximately 250m)

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **1st Choice** | **Chumphon standard clones** — proven in hot lowlands |
| **2nd Choice** | **Local mixed Robusta** — lower cost but variable quality |
| **Expected Yield** | 300-500 kg green bean/rai (highest potential) |
| **Market Price** | Commodity: 40-70 THB/kg; Processed: 80-150 THB/kg |
| **ROI Timeline** | 3 years |

**Below 400m, Arabica is NOT viable — do not attempt.**

## Decision Factors Deep Dive

### Factor 1: Altitude (Primary Determinant)

| Altitude Zone | Arabica | Robusta | Primary Factor |
|--------------|---------|---------|----------------|
| More than 1,000m | Excellent | Poor (too cold) | Cool nights = slow cherry maturation = complex sugars |
| 800-1,000m | Good | Marginal | Sweet spot for Thai-developed varieties |
| 600-800m | Marginal | Good | Decision boundary — site-specific assessment needed |
| 400-600m | Poor | Excellent | Heat stress limits Arabica |
| 200-400m | Not viable | Excellent | Robusta dominates |

**Altitude-temperature relationship in Northern Thailand:**
- Temperature drops approximately 6.5 degrees C per 1,000m elevation gain
- At 1,200m: avg 18-22 degrees C (ideal Arabica range)
- At 800m: avg 22-26 degrees C (upper limit for quality Arabica)
- At 500m: avg 25-30 degrees C (Robusta territory)
- At 300m: avg 28-33 degrees C (lowland Robusta only)

### Factor 2: Temperature

| Temperature Range (avg) | Recommended Species | Notes |
|------------------------|--------------------|----|
| 15-22 degrees C | Arabica | Ideal — slow maturation, best quality |
| 22-25 degrees C | Arabica with shade | Requires shade management, rust monitoring |
| 25-28 degrees C | Robusta or Catimor | Catimor at upper end only; Robusta preferred |
| 28-35 degrees C | Robusta only | Ensure adequate water supply |

### Factor 3: Rainfall

| Rainfall (mm/year) | Status | Action Required |
|--------------------|--------|-----------------|
| 1,200-1,800 | Ideal for Arabica | No irrigation needed |
| 1,800-2,500 | Good for Robusta | Ensure drainage |
| Below 1,200 | Deficit | Irrigation REQUIRED |

**Northern Thailand rainfall patterns:** Wet season May-October (80% of annual rainfall); Dry season November-April. Total annual: 1,000-2,000mm. Climate change impact: Rainfall becoming more unpredictable — harvest season (Nov) increasingly wet, causing quality issues and mold.

### Factor 4: Soil Type and pH

| Parameter | Arabica Ideal | Robusta Ideal | Thai Highland Soils |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **pH** | 5.5-6.0 | 5.0-6.5 | Typically 4.5-5.5 (needs liming) |
| **Organic Matter** | More than 3% | More than 2% | Variable; often low on former opium plots |
| **Drainage** | Well-drained | Tolerates heavier soils | Slope provides natural drainage |

**Action items:** Test soil pH before planting; apply agricultural lime (dolomite) if pH less than 5.0; add organic compost to improve structure.

### Factor 5: Slope and Aspect

| Factor | North-Facing Slope | South-Facing Slope |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Temperature** | Cooler (approximately -2-3 degrees C) | Warmer (approximately +2-3 degrees C) |
| **Arabica Suitability** | Better at lower altitudes | Needs 100m higher altitude |
| **Robusta Suitability** | Too cool below 600m | Good |

### Factor 6: Farmer's Market Target

| Market Target | Variety Focus | Altitude | Price Range (THB/kg green bean) |
|--------------|--------------|----------|-------------------------------|
| Specialty competition | Geisha, Typica, Bourbon | More than 1,000m | 500-1,000+ |
| Specialty local/roaster | Chiang Mai 80, Caturra, Catuai | 800-1,000m | 280-400 |
| Specialty Robusta | Chumphon specialty clone, FRT | 400-600m | 150-280 |
| Commodity Arabica | Catimor | 600-1,000m | 80-120 |
| Commodity Robusta | Chumphon standard | 200-500m | 40-70 |

### Factor 7: Farmer's Budget and Risk Tolerance

| Budget Level | Recommendation | Investment/rai | Risk Level |
|-------------|---------------|----------------------|------------|
| **Low (less than 15,000 THB/rai)** | Robusta commodity | 10,000-15,000 THB | Low |
| **Medium (15,000-30,000 THB/rai)** | Catimor Arabica or specialty Robusta | 18,000-30,000 THB | Medium |
| **High (30,000-60,000 THB/rai)** | Specialty Arabica (Chiang Mai 80, Caturra) | 35,000-60,000 THB | Medium-High |
| **Premium (more than 60,000 THB/rai)** | Geisha, Bourbon, competition-grade | 60,000-120,000+ THB | High |

### Factor 8: Availability of Shade Trees

| Shade Situation | Arabica Recommendation | Robusta Recommendation |
|----------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Existing forest shade** | Typica, Bourbon, Geisha | Any Robusta variety |
| **Miang tea trees (traditional)** | Catimor, Chiang Mai 80 | Not applicable |
| **Plant shade (Leucaena, Gliricidia)** | Catimor, Caturra | Chumphon, FRT |
| **No shade available** | NOT recommended at less than 1,000m | Robusta with limited shade |

### Factor 9: Distance to Processing Facility

| Distance | Arabica Impact | Robusta Impact | Recommendation |
|----------|---------------|---------------|----------------|
| Less than 5 km | Ideal — same-day processing | Not critical | Any variety, washed processing |
| 5-20 km | Good — processing within 12 hrs | Acceptable | Hardy varieties, honey/natural process |
| 20-50 km | Risky — quality degradation | Acceptable | Natural/dry process only |
| More than 50 km | High risk | Manageable | Robusta preferred; invest in on-farm pulping |

### Factor 10: Farmer's Experience Level

| Experience Level | Recommended Path | Varieties |
|-----------------|-----------------|-----------|
| **New farmer (0-2 years)** | Start with Robusta | Chumphon, FRT |
| **Some experience (2-5 years)** | Catimor Arabica or specialty Robusta | Chiang Mai 80, TR11 |
| **Experienced (5-10 years)** | Specialty Arabica | Chiang Mai 80, Caturra, Catuai |
| **Expert (more than 10 years)** | Premium/heirloom varieties | Typica, Bourbon, Geisha, Pacamara |

## Financial Comparison Table

| Parameter | Arabica Specialty (Catimor at 1,000m) | Arabica Commodity (Catimor at 700m) | Robusta Specialty (Chumphon at 500m) | Robusta Commodity (Chumphon at 300m) |
|-----------|--------------------------------------|-------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| **Setup Cost/rai** | 35,000-50,000 THB | 25,000-35,000 THB | 20,000-30,000 THB | 12,000-18,000 THB |
| **Annual Cost/rai** | 8,000-12,000 THB | 6,000-9,000 THB | 5,000-8,000 THB | 3,000-6,000 THB |
| **Yield/rai (kg)** | 100-180 | 80-130 | 250-350 | 300-500 |
| **Price/kg (THB)** | 280-400 | 80-120 | 150-280 | 40-70 |
| **Gross Income/rai/yr** | 28,000-72,000 | 6,400-15,600 | 37,500-98,000 | 12,000-35,000 |
| **Net Income/rai/yr** | 16,000-60,000 | (2,600)-6,600 | 29,500-90,000 | 6,000-29,000 |

**Key Financial Insight:** Arabica commodity at marginal altitude (700m) can be unprofitable — the gap between specialty and commodity prices makes altitude and quality the critical factors. Robusta specialty offers the most consistent returns per rai due to higher yields AND improving market perception.

## Impact of Climate Change on Altitude Suitability

| Impact | Current (2025) | Projected (2030-2040) | Farmer Response |
|--------|---------------|----------------------|----------------|
| **Temperature rise** | +0.5-1.0 degrees C since 2000 | Additional +0.5-1.5 degrees C | Shift Arabica zone upward by 100-200m |
| **Altitude zone shift** | 800m boundary | Could move to 900m+ | Current 600-800m zone becoming marginal for Arabica |
| **Unpredictable rainfall** | Nov rains disrupting harvest | More frequent and severe | Invest in covered drying; adjust harvest timing |
| **Leaf rust expansion** | Already severe at 800m | Could affect 1,000m+ | Resistant varieties (Catimor, Chiang Mai 80) essential |

**Adaptation strategies:**
1. Plant shade trees NOW — 30+ year old shade-grown farms are more resilient
2. Shift to rust-resistant varieties (Chiang Mai 80, Catimor) at 800-1,000m
3. Invest in covered drying infrastructure for unpredictable wet seasons
4. Diversify with intercropping (macadamia, miang tea, fruit trees)
5. Consider moving Arabica operations to higher elevations if possible
6. Explore specialty Robusta as climate-adapted alternative at 600-800m

## Case Studies

### Case Study 1: Doi Chang (Chiang Rai)

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Altitude** | 1,000-1,700m |
| **Community** | Akha hill tribe |

## Action Checklist for New Coffee Farmers

- [ ] **Measure altitude** accurately using GPS (not estimated from maps)
- [ ] **Test soil pH** — send sample to local agricultural office (free or low cost)
- [ ] **Assess shade availability** — existing trees? Miang tea? Need to plant?
- [ ] **Calculate budget** — can you afford 3-4 years before income?
- [ ] **Identify nearest processing facility** — distance and transport options
- [ ] **Contact local Royal Project or DOAE office** — free seedlings and advice often available
- [ ] **Visit successful farms** at similar altitude — learn from neighbors
- [ ] **Choose 1-2 varieties maximum** — don't mix too many; focus on what works
- [ ] **Plan shade trees first** — plant 6-12 months before coffee seedlings
- [ ] **Start small** — 5-10 rai maximum for first planting; expand after success
