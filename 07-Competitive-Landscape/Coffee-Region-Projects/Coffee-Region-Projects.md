# Coffee Region Projects in Northern Thailand

> **Summary**: Analysis of established coffee and agriculture projects operating in Northern Thailand's highlands — Doi Tung Development Project, Royal Project Foundation, and Kiao Farming — their technology adoption, IoT capabilities, and potential as partners rather than competitors for Prime My B.

---

## Overview

Northern Thailand's highlands are home to several major agricultural development projects that share Prime My B's geographic focus and, in some cases, its coffee focus. The Doi Tung Development Project and Royal Project Foundation are Thailand's most prominent Arabica coffee institutions, operating across Chiang Rai, Chiang Mai, Mae Hong Son, and Nan provinces — the same provinces where Prime My B would deploy. However, these projects are fundamentally **social development organizations**, not technology companies. They lack IoT sensor platforms, data dashboards, and predictive analytics. This creates a natural partnership opportunity: Prime My B provides the technology layer that these established coffee institutions need, while they provide the farmer networks, coffee expertise, and institutional credibility that Prime My B needs for adoption. Understanding these projects' current technology posture and unmet needs is essential for Prime My B's partnership strategy.

## Doi Tung Development Project / Mae Fah Luang Foundation

### Profile

The Doi Tung Development Project, established in 1988 by HRH Princess Srinagarindara (the Princess Mother), is one of the world's most celebrated sustainable development projects. It transformed the Golden Triangle from opium production to Arabica coffee farming and now supports approximately 30 villages and 10,000+ people across Chiang Rai Province. Doi Tung Coffee holds GI (Geographical Indication) registration since 2006 and is one of Thailand's most recognized specialty coffee brands.

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Founded** | 1988 |
| **Organization** | Mae Fah Luang Foundation under Royal Patronage |
| **Location** | Doi Tung, Chiang Rai Province |
| **Altitude** | 800-1,400 masl |
| **Coffee Type** | 100% Arabica |
| **Community** | ~30 villages, 10,000+ people |
| **GI Registration** | Doi Tung Coffee (2006) |
| **Website** | doitung.com, maefahluang.org |

### Current Technology Posture

Doi Tung's operations are rooted in sustainable farming practices, community enterprise, and social development rather than technology-driven monitoring. The project mentions "precision agriculture" in its strategic documents, but no dedicated IoT sensor platform or data analytics system has been deployed. Current technology use includes:

| Technology Area | Status | Notes |
|----------------|--------|-------|
| Soil testing | ⚠️ Periodic | Laboratory-based; not continuous |
| Weather monitoring | ⚠️ Basic | Regional weather data, not farm-specific |
| Crop health assessment | ⚠️ Manual | Visual inspection by agricultural extension staff |
| Yield tracking | ⚠️ Manual | Post-harvest records; no predictive modeling |
| Quality assessment | ⚠️ Post-harvest | Cupping after processing; no pre-harvest quality indicators |
| Irrigation management | ❌ | Rain-fed cultivation; no sensor-driven irrigation |
| Disease early warning | ❌ | No CLR prediction system |
| Data dashboard | ❌ | No centralized farm monitoring system |

### IoT Opportunity Analysis

Doi Tung's coffee operations present a clear IoT opportunity because their existing farming practices align perfectly with sensor-driven optimization:

| Prime My B Capability | Doi Tung Need | Impact |
|----------------------|--------------|--------|
| CLR prediction (leaf wetness + temp + humidity) | HIGH — CLR is a major risk at their altitude range | Early warning enables preventive spraying, saving 30-50% of potential crop loss |
| Shade management (PPFD monitoring) | HIGH — shade-grown Arabica requires precise shade % | Sensor-verified shade levels ensure optimal photosynthesis |
| Yield prediction | MODERATE — they track yields manually but cannot forecast | Predictive yield helps processing capacity planning and market commitments |
| Quality prediction | HIGH — specialty-grade Doi Tung Coffee commands premium pricing | Pre-harvest quality indicators enable selective harvesting and processing decisions |
| Soil moisture monitoring | HIGH — rain-fed cultivation makes drought monitoring critical | Drought early warning protects cherry quality during dry periods |

### Partnership Potential

| Partnership Type | Feasibility | Value for Prime My B | Value for Doi Tung |
|-----------------|------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Pilot deployment** | HIGH | Real-world validation in Thailand's most famous coffee region | Free IoT monitoring for pilot farms |
| **Data sharing** | MODERATE | Access to 30+ years of yield and quality data for model training | Advanced analytics on their historical data |
| **Co-branding** | MODERATE | "Doi Tung + Prime My B" carries enormous credibility | Technology brand association |
| **Joint research** | HIGH | Academic publication and grant eligibility | Innovation recognition |
| **Full deployment** | LONG-TERM | Scaled deployment across 30+ villages | Comprehensive farm monitoring |

**Key Insight:** Doi Tung is the ideal pilot partner for Prime My B. A successful pilot on Doi Tung's coffee farms — demonstrating CLR early warning, yield prediction, and quality indicators — would provide more market validation than any amount of marketing. Doi Tung's GI-registered brand and international recognition would lend Prime My B immediate credibility in the specialty coffee market.

## Royal Project Foundation — Smart Farming Adoption

### Profile

The Royal Project Foundation, established in 1969 under the initiative of H.M. King Bhumibol Adulyadej, is the single most important institution for Arabica coffee development in Northern Thailand. With 22 research and development centers across the highlands and annual coffee production of approximately 500 tons, the Royal Project provides seedling distribution, guaranteed purchase programs, technical training, and market access through the "Doi Kham" brand.

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Founded** | 1969 |
| **Headquarters** | Chiang Mai |
| **Coffee Focus** | 100% Arabica |
| **Annual Production** | ~500 tons across 22 areas |
| **Key Varieties** | RPF-C3, RPF-C4, plus 4 more registered varieties (2021) |
| **Website** | royalproject.org |

### Smart Farming Initiatives

Unlike Doi Tung, the Royal Project Foundation is actively adopting smart farming technology through a partnership with Taiwan's ICDF (International Cooperation and Development Fund) and Kiao Farming Company. This makes the Royal Project a more technology-forward potential partner.

| Initiative | Partner | Technology | Status |
|-----------|---------|-----------|--------|
| **Smart farming systems** | Taiwan ICDF + Kiao Farming | IoT control boxes for irrigation/fertigation | Active deployment |
| **Online video support** | Kiao Farming | Remote monitoring and training via video | Active |
| **Smart control boxes** | Kiao Farming | Mobile phone control of irrigation and fertilizer | Being deployed |
| **Personnel training** | Kiao Farming | Building RPF staff capability in smart farming | Ongoing |

### Technology Gap Analysis

While the Royal Project is adopting IoT hardware (via Kiao), they are missing the analytics and prediction layer that Prime My B would provide:

| Capability | Kiao/Royal Project | Prime My B Add-on |
|-----------|-------------------|------------------|
| Irrigation control | ✅ (Kiao control box) | Sensor-optimized scheduling |
| Fertilizer control | ✅ (Kiao control box) | Sensor-driven fertigation timing |
| Remote monitoring | ✅ (Video) | Continuous sensor data streaming |
| CLR prediction | ❌ | Leaf wetness + temperature + humidity model |
| Yield prediction | ❌ | Multi-factor yield forecast |
| Quality prediction | ❌ | Pre-harvest quality indicators |
| LINE alerts | ❌ | Thai-language actionable alerts |
| Coffee-specific models | ❌ | Variety-specific thresholds |

**Key Insight:** The Royal Project + Kiao partnership validates that IoT technology is desired by Thailand's premier coffee institutions, but Kiao's control-box approach is hardware-centric (irrigation/fertigation control) without the analytics layer. Prime My B could complement Kiao's hardware by providing the intelligence layer — coffee-specific models that determine WHEN and HOW MUCH to irrigate/fertigate, based on real-time sensor data and coffee physiology. This positions Prime My B as the "brain" on top of Kiao's "hands."

### Partnership Potential

| Partnership Type | Feasibility | Value for Prime My B | Value for Royal Project |
|-----------------|------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Analytics layer for Kiao** | HIGH | Integration with deployed hardware; large farmer network | Intelligence layer for their existing IoT investment |
| **Pilot at RPF centers** | HIGH | Access to 22 research centers with controlled conditions | Advanced monitoring at minimal additional cost |
| **Variety-specific model development** | VERY HIGH | RPF-C3/C4 sensor profiles for [[Variety-Sensor-Profiles]] | Data-driven variety management |
| **Joint training programs** | MODERATE | Access to RPF training infrastructure | Technology curriculum addition |
| **Data sharing for models** | HIGH | 50+ years of coffee production data | Modern data analytics on historical records |

## Kiao Farming Company (Taiwanese, Operating in Thailand)

### Profile

Kiao Farming Company is a Taiwanese agricultural IoT startup that provides smart control boxes and agricultural IoT information systems. They are currently operating in Northern Thailand through their partnership with the Royal Project Foundation, bringing Taiwan's advanced precision agriculture technology to Thailand's highland farming communities.

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Type** | Agricultural IoT information systems |
| **Website** | kiaofarming.com/en |
| **Origin** | Taiwan |
| **Thailand Partner** | Royal Project Foundation |
| **Technology** | Smart control box + IoT sensor integration |

### Capabilities

Kiao's approach is hardware-centric, focusing on providing farmers with physical control over irrigation and fertilization through a mobile phone interface. Their system uses visual program blocks for flexible, programmable control of farm operations.

| Feature | Available | Technology |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Smart control box | ✅ | Mobile phone-controlled irrigation and fertigation |
| Visual program blocks | ✅ | Drag-and-drop programming for farm automation |
| Agricultural IoT info system | ✅ | Sensor data collection and display |
| Online video support | ✅ | Remote training and monitoring |
| Yield prediction | ❌ | Not offered |
| Quality prediction | ❌ | Not offered |
| Coffee-specific models | ❌ | General horticultural focus |
| Thai language | ⚠️ | Through Royal Project localization |
| LINE integration | ❌ | Own mobile interface |

### Overlap Assessment

| Dimension | Overlap Level | Explanation |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| IoT Hardware | MODERATE | Control box approach (not sensor nodes) |
| Dashboard | LOW | Mobile control interface, not analytics dashboard |
| Yield Prediction | NONE | Not offered |
| Quality Prediction | NONE | Not offered |
| Coffee Focus | INDIRECT | Through Royal Project; general horticultural |
| N. Thailand Presence | HIGH | Active through Royal Project partnership |

**Key Insight:** Kiao is a **complementary technology provider**, not a competitor. Their control box handles the "actuation" side (turning irrigation/fertigation on and off), while Prime My B would handle the "intelligence" side (deciding when and how much based on coffee-specific models). The ideal architecture would be: Prime My B sensors + models → Prime My B decision engine → Kiao control box → physical irrigation/fertigation. This integration would give Royal Project farmers a complete sensor-to-actuation pipeline.

## Academic Research — Coffee Yield Prediction in Northern Thailand

Beyond institutional projects, academic research in Northern Thailand provides evidence that coffee yield and quality prediction using data-driven methods is both feasible and actively studied. This research base could inform Prime My B's prediction models and provides academic credibility for the platform.

### Key Studies

| Study | Method | Focus | Finding |
|-------|--------|-------|---------|
| "Forecasting arabica coffee yields by ARIMA" (AIMS Press, 2023) | ARIMA time series | Arabica cherry production in Northern Thailand | ARIMA models can forecast regional coffee production with reasonable accuracy |
| "Prediction of arabica coffee production using ANN" (PMC/NIH, 2022) | ANN + Multiple Linear Regression | Arabica coffee yield prediction | Neural networks outperform linear regression for yield prediction; "crop yield and its prediction are crucial in agricultural production planning" |
| "Arabica Coffee Growing in Chiang Rai and Chiang Mai" (ResearchGate, 2024) | Sustainability analysis | Transformation from opium to coffee | Documents the sustainability challenges that IoT monitoring could address |

### Implications for Prime My B

These studies demonstrate that:

1. **Academic research validates the demand** for coffee yield prediction in Northern Thailand — researchers are actively studying this problem because the agricultural community needs it.
2. **ARIMA and ANN models are starting points** — Prime My B could begin with these proven approaches and then improve with IoT sensor data that these studies did not have access to.
3. **No commercial product has operationalized this research** — the gap between academic research and farmer-accessible tools is precisely where Prime My B would operate.
4. **Regional specificity matters** — these studies focus on Northern Thailand specifically, confirming that national-level platforms (HandySense) cannot provide the regional specialization that coffee requires.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Doi Tung is the ideal pilot partner** — same region, same crop, no existing IoT, strong brand recognition. A successful pilot there would validate Prime My B better than any other partnership.
2. **Royal Project + Kiao is an integration opportunity** — Kiao provides the hardware control layer; Prime My B provides the intelligence layer. Together they form a complete sensor-to-actuation system for coffee.
3. **No project in Northern Thailand's coffee region has IoT analytics** — the gap is clear. Existing projects either have no technology (Doi Tung) or have hardware control only (Royal Project + Kiao), but none have predictive analytics.
4. **Academic research provides a foundation** — ARIMA and ANN models for coffee yield prediction in Northern Thailand have been published. Prime My B can build on these with real-time IoT sensor data that these studies lacked.
5. **Partnership, not competition** — every project in this region would benefit from Prime My B's capabilities. The positioning should always be "we help you do what you do better" rather than "we compete with you."
