**Key Question**: "What existing products or projects in Thailand already do what Prime My B aims to do — and where are the gaps that Prime My B can fill?"

---

**Key Insights from Phase 7**:

- **HandySense B-Farm** (NECTEC/NSTDA) is the highest-overlap government platform — open-source IoT + AI + dashboard, but **crop-agnostic** with no coffee-specific models, no yield prediction, and no quality prediction
- **NECTEC Predictive Farming Platform** is the only government project explicitly targeting yield prediction, but it remains in research/infrastructure phase and is not coffee-specific
- **Ricult** (Gates Foundation-backed) offers free yield prediction via satellite data, but uses **no ground-level IoT sensors** and has no coffee focus
- **SPsmartplants** proves the **crop-specific IoT + LINE integration model works in Thailand** (durian-focused), but covers no coffee and offers no yield/quality prediction
- **No existing product in Thailand** offers coffee-specific yield prediction, coffee quality prediction, or coffee disease prediction using IoT sensor data — this is the clear market gap
- **Doi Tung and Royal Project** are potential **partners, not competitors** — they operate in the same Northern Thailand coffee region but lack IoT platforms
- The competitive landscape is fragmented: government platforms are free but generic; commercial products are crop-agnostic or focused on rice/durian; no one serves the coffee IoT niche in Northern Thailand
- Academic research exists for coffee yield prediction in Northern Thailand (ARIMA, ANN models), but **no commercial product** has operationalized this research
