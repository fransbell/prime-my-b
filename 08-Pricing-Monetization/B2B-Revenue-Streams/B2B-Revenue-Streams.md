# B2B Revenue Streams

> **Summary**: Detailed analysis of five B2B revenue streams that can sustain Prime My B while keeping farmer services free — coffee exporters/buyers (primary), financial institutions, input suppliers, government/research, and data licensing — with specific pricing models, value propositions, and revenue projections for each.

---

## Overview

B2B revenue is the engine that makes Prime My B's free-for-farmer model work. While individual farmers cannot afford meaningful subscriptions, the businesses and institutions in the coffee value chain can easily pay for intelligence that improves their operations. A single specialty coffee exporter handling 10 tons per year at 350 THB/kg generates 3.5 million THB in revenue — a 5,000 THB/month subscription for quality traceability data is 0.17% of their revenue. A bank providing a 500,000 THB farm loan at 5% interest earns 25,000 THB per year — a 1,000 THB farm credit assessment fee is 4% of one year's interest income. These economics make B2B monetization both feasible and attractive, as long as Prime My B delivers genuine value through its unique coffee intelligence capabilities.

## Revenue Stream 1: Coffee Exporters & Specialty Buyers

### Value Proposition

Coffee exporters and specialty buyers are the primary B2B revenue stream because Prime My B's data directly addresses their biggest challenges: verifying coffee quality before purchase, tracing coffee origin for certification, and forecasting supply availability for contract planning. Currently, exporters must physically visit farms or rely on farmer self-reporting for quality assessment. Prime My B provides continuous, sensor-verified quality data that reduces information asymmetry and procurement risk.

### Customer Segments

| Segment | Example | Annual Volume | Pain Point | Prime My B Value |
|---------|---------|--------------|------------|-----------------|
| **Large exporters** | CoffeeWORKS, BOWA, VMAC | 50+ tons/year | Quality consistency across many farms | Standardized sensor-verified quality data |
| **Specialty roasters** | Thailand specialty roasters | 1-10 tons/year | Sourcing high-quality beans; origin stories | Farm-level quality predictions; traceability |
| **International buyers** | Japanese/EU specialty importers | 5-50 tons/year | Traceability for EU PGI compliance; ESG | Full sensor data trail from farm to export |
| **Cooperative processors** | Doi Tung, Royal Project | 10-100 tons/year | Processing capacity planning; quality sorting | Yield forecasts; quality predictions by zone |

### Pricing Model

| Package | Monthly Price (THB) | Farms Covered | Features |
|---------|---------------------|---------------|----------|
| **Single Origin** | 5,000 | Up to 20 farms | Quality predictions, yield forecasts, sensor data access |
| **Multi-Origin** | 15,000 | Up to 100 farms | Above + regional analytics, supply forecasting, API access |
| **Enterprise** | 30,000-50,000 | Unlimited | Above + custom integrations, white-label dashboard, priority support |
| **Per-report** | 500-2,000 | Per farm assessment | One-time quality/yield report for specific farm |

### Revenue Projection

| Year | Customers | Avg. Monthly Revenue | Annual Revenue |
|------|-----------|---------------------|---------------|
| Year 1 | 1 pilot | 5,000 | 30,000 (6 months) |
| Year 2 | 5 (3 exporters, 2 buyers) | 8,000 avg | 480,000 |
| Year 3 | 15 (10 exporters, 5 buyers) | 10,000 avg | 1,800,000 |

### What Makes This Work

Coffee quality prediction is the key differentiator. An exporter who can verify that a farm's sensor data predicts SCA 82+ (specialty grade) before purchasing has a significant competitive advantage in the specialty market. This is data that no other platform in Thailand can provide.

## Revenue Stream 2: Financial Institutions

### Value Proposition

Banks and financial institutions need reliable farm-level data to assess credit risk for agricultural loans. Currently, BAAC relies on self-reported farm data and basic land title verification. Prime My B provides sensor-verified farm performance data — historical yields, disease risk profiles, irrigation reliability — that enables more accurate credit scoring and potentially lower interest rates for data-verified farms.

### Customer Segments

| Segment | Example | Loan Size | Prime My B Value |
|---------|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| **BAAC** | Government agricultural bank | 100,000-3,000,000 THB | Farm performance data for loan assessment |
| **Commercial banks** | Kasikorn, SCB agricultural lending | 500,000-5,000,000 THB | Risk assessment for coffee farm mortgages |
| **Microfinance** | Thai microfinance institutions | 10,000-100,000 THB | Quick credit scoring for small farmers |
| **Insurance companies** | Thai crop insurance (emerging) | Parametric coverage | Sensor data for parametric insurance triggers |

### Pricing Model

| Service | Price | Customer | Frequency |
|---------|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Farm credit assessment** | 500-2,000 THB | Banks | Per loan application |
| **Portfolio risk monitoring** | 5,000-15,000 THB/month | BAAC, commercial banks | Continuous |
| **Parametric insurance data** | 2,000-5,000 THB/month | Insurance companies | Continuous |
| **Loan performance tracking** | 200-500 THB/farm/year | Banks | Annual |

### The BAAC Opportunity

BAAC's Community Enterprise Loan at 0.01% interest is the most attractive financial product for coffee farmer groups. Prime My B can provide the farm performance data that makes these loans less risky for BAAC:

- **Before loan**: Prime My B report shows sensor-verified farm conditions, historical yield data, CLR risk profile
- **During loan**: Ongoing monitoring confirms farm is well-managed (sensor data shows irrigation, fertilization, disease management)
- **After loan**: Yield and quality predictions demonstrate repayment capacity

This could become a self-reinforcing cycle: farmers use Prime My B → better loan assessment → lower interest rates → higher farm investment → better sensor data → more valuable B2B data.

### Revenue Projection

| Year | Assessments | Revenue per Assessment | Annual Revenue |
|------|------------|----------------------|---------------|
| Year 1 | 20 | 500 THB | 10,000 |
| Year 2 | 50 | 750 THB | 37,500 + 60,000 portfolio monitoring |
| Year 3 | 200 | 1,000 THB | 200,000 + 180,000 portfolio monitoring |

## Revenue Stream 3: Input Suppliers

### Value Proposition

Agricultural input suppliers (fertilizer, pesticide, equipment companies) want direct access to coffee farmers at the moment they make purchasing decisions. Prime My B's sensor data and decision logic identify exactly when a farmer needs inputs — when CLR risk is HIGH and fungicide is needed, when soil pH is low and lime is needed, when irrigation is insufficient and drip kit is needed. This is the most valuable moment for an input supplier to reach a farmer, and Prime My B can facilitate that connection.

### Customer Segments

| Segment | Example | Product | Prime My B Value |
|---------|---------|---------|-----------------|
| **Fertilizer companies** | Yara Thailand, Thai Central Chemical | NPK blends | Soil sensor data identifies nutrient needs |
| **Pesticide/fungicide** | Syngenta, Bayer Thailand | CLR fungicides | Leaf wetness data identifies spray timing |
| **Irrigation suppliers** | Netafim, Kanok Group | Drip systems | Soil moisture data identifies irrigation need |
| **Equipment suppliers** | Sun Roaster, CoffeeWORKS | Processing equipment | Quality data identifies processing investment opportunity |
| **Sensor hardware** | Gravitech, ArtronShop | IoT components | Direct sales through Prime My B platform |

### Pricing Model

| Model | Price | Details |
|-------|-------|---------|
| **Marketplace commission** | 5-15% | On input sales facilitated through Prime My B |
| **Sponsored recommendations** | 1,000-5,000 THB/month | Input supplier sponsors recommendation in LINE alerts |
| **Demand signals** | 2,000-10,000 THB/month | Aggregated data on farmer input needs by region |
| **Direct access (limited)** | 500-2,000 THB/month | Verified farmer contact for regional sales teams |

### Ethical Considerations

Input supplier monetization must be handled carefully to maintain farmer trust:

1. **Recommendations must be genuine** — never recommend an input that sensor data doesn't support
2. **Multiple suppliers** — always offer 2-3 options, not just the paying sponsor
3. **Clear disclosure** — farmers should know when a recommendation is sponsored
4. **No pay-to-play** — input quality and relevance must be the primary selection criteria

### Revenue Projection

| Year | Revenue Source | Annual Revenue |
|------|---------------|---------------|
| Year 1 | None (building trust first) | 0 |
| Year 2 | Marketplace pilot + demand signals | 100,000 |
| Year 3 | Full marketplace + sponsored content | 300,000 |

## Revenue Stream 5: Data Licensing & Emerging Opportunities

### Value Proposition

Prime My B generates a unique dataset: continuous, sensor-verified, coffee-specific microclimate and crop performance data from Northern Thailand. This data is valuable to entities that need coffee market intelligence but cannot obtain it from any other source.

### Data Monetization Opportunities

| Buyer | Data Product | Price | Maturity |
|-------|-------------|-------|----------|
| **Commodity traders** | Regional yield forecasts | 10,000-50,000 THB/month | EMERGING |
| **Insurance companies** | Parametric trigger data | 2,000-5,000 THB/month | PILOT |
| **Carbon credit markets** | Agroforestry carbon sequestration data | Per-tonne carbon credit | EMERGING |
| **Coffee certification bodies** | Traceability data for organic/fair trade | 500-1,000 THB/farm/year | VIABLE |
| **Real estate/land valuation** | Farm productivity data | Per-report | FUTURE |

### Critical Data Ethics

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Farmer data ownership** | Farmers own their raw data; Prime My B licenses aggregated/anonymized insights |
| **Opt-in required** | Farmers must explicitly consent to B2B data sharing |
| **Anonymization** | Individual farm data is never shared; only regional aggregates and trends |
| **Revenue sharing** | Consider sharing 10-20% of data licensing revenue with participating farmers |
| **Transparency** | Annual data practices report; clear explanation of what is shared with whom |

### Revenue Projection

| Year | Revenue Source | Annual Revenue |
|------|---------------|---------------|
| Year 1 | None (insufficient data) | 0 |
| Year 2 | Pilot data licensing | 50,000 |
| Year 3 | Full data products + carbon credits | 200,000 |

## Key Takeaways

1. **Coffee exporters are the primary B2B customer** — they have the budget, the need, and the willingness to pay for quality traceability and yield forecasting. Start here.
2. **Government contracts are the fastest revenue** — DEPA OTOD deployments can generate 200,000 THB per community with minimal sales effort. Use these to fund operations while building exporter relationships.
3. **Financial institutions are a long-term opportunity** — BAAC credit assessment could become significant, but requires proven prediction accuracy over multiple seasons.
4. **Input supplier monetization requires trust** — do not launch marketplace features until farmer trust is established (Year 2+). Ethical concerns could undermine the entire platform.
5. **Data licensing is emerging, not immediate** — the dataset becomes more valuable with scale and time. Focus on data quality and volume first; monetize later.
6. **All B2B revenue depends on farmer adoption** — without farmers generating sensor data, there is nothing to sell. The free farmer tier is not charity; it is the data acquisition mechanism that enables every B2B stream.
